Crowdfunding Poverty

1. Direct Cash Transfers

Donations go directly to impoverished individuals, who use the funds for basic needs, education, or starting small businesses.
Impact: Studies show cash transfers improve food security, healthcare, and long-term economic stability
2. Medical Crowdfunding

Example:GoFundMe, Milaap, Ketto
How It Works: Individuals raise funds for urgent medical expenses, surgeries, and treatments.
Impact: Prevents medical bankruptcy and ensures healthcare access for low-income families.
3. Education & Scholarships

Example: Scholarship crowdfunding campaigns on platforms like GoFundMe and Indiegogo
How It Works: Students from poor backgrounds raise funds for tuition, books, and living expenses.
Impact: Helps break the cycle of poverty through higher education.
4. Small Business & Entrepreneurship Support

Example: Kiva (microloans), Kickstarter (small businesses)
How It Works: Entrepreneurs in poverty-stricken areas receive small loans or grants to start businesses.
Impact: Creates employment, boosts local economies, and increases self-sufficiency.
5. Emergency & Disaster Relief

Example: GlobalGiving, GoFundMe for disaster victims
How It Works: People donate to families affected by natural disasters, war, or crises.
Impact: Provides immediate financial relief for rebuilding homes, buying essentials, and recovering livelihoods.
6. Community Development Projects

Example: Patreon and GoFundMe for social causes
How It Works: Funds go towards building schools, wells, healthcare facilities, and community programs.
Impact: Long-term poverty reduction through infrastructure development and social support.
7. Housing & Shelter Initiatives

Example: Habitat for Humanity’s crowdfunding efforts
How It Works: Funds are raised to build affordable housing for low-income families.
Impact: Provides safe, stable housing, reducing homelessness and improving living conditions.
8. Technology & Internet Access

Example: One Laptop per Child (OLPC) and community internet projects
How It Works: Crowdfunding supports tech access, digital literacy, and internet connectivity for poor communities.
Impact: Improves education, job opportunities, and access to global markets.
9. Water & Sanitation Projects

Example:charity: water (crowdfunded clean water projects)
How It Works: Donations fund wells, water filtration systems, and sanitation projects in impoverished regions.
Impact: Reduces waterborne diseases and improves quality of life.
10. Sustainable Farming & Food Security

Example: Crowdfarming initiatives for small-scale farmers
How It Works: Supports farmers with seeds, equipment, and training to boost agricultural productivity.
Impact: Increases food security and income for rural families.
11. Supporting Refugees & Displaced People

Example: GoFundMe campaigns for refugee families
How It Works: Funds provide housing, legal aid, job training, and basic necessities for displaced individuals.
Impact: Helps refugees rebuild their lives in new environments.
12. Mental Health & Social Services

Example:Therapy and support group Crowdfunding
How It Works: Funds mental health services for those who can’t afford therapy or counseling.
Impact:Improves well-being, job stability, and overall quality of life.
